Exploring the Length:

Density Plot of Length

The density plot of text lengths illustrates the distribution of lengths for both human-written and AI-generated texts within our dataset. On the x-axis, we have the length of the texts, while the y-axis represents the density. The blue curve corresponds to human-written texts (denoted as '0'), and the orange curve corresponds to AI-generated texts (denoted as '1'). From the plot, it is evident that AI-generated texts tend to be shorter, with a higher peak around shorter lengths compared to human-written texts. The distribution of human-written texts is broader and extends further, indicating a wider range of text lengths. This visualization helps us understand the inherent differences in the length of texts based on their origin, providing insights into stylistic characteristics.

BoxPlot of Length

The boxplot provides a summary of the length distribution of texts in the dataset. The x-axis represents the length of the texts, while the y-axis shows the value range. The box represents the interquartile range (IQR) where the middle 50% of the data points lie, with the line inside the box indicating the median length. The whiskers extend to the smallest and largest values within 1.5 times the IQR from the lower and upper quartiles, respectively. Points outside this range are considered outliers. The boxplot shows that the majority of text lengths fall within a specific range, with a median length around 1000 characters. There are numerous outliers extending beyond 3000 characters, suggesting the presence of significantly longer texts in the dataset. This visualization provides a clear overview of the central tendency, variability, and potential outliers in text lengths.

Exploring the Word Count:

Density Plot of Word Count

This boxplot displays the distribution of word counts across the dataset. The x-axis represents the word count, while the y-axis shows the value range. The majority of texts contain between 100 and 300 words, with some outliers having up to 800 words. This chart helps us understand the typical word count of texts and highlights any particularly lengthy texts in terms of word count.

BoxPlot of Word Count
